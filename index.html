Welcome

I am a physics student with a major interested in exploring different subfields, including high energy physics, condensed matter physics and astrophysics. Currently, I am an undergraduate research assistant at Academia Sinica, where I build and apply theoretical and numerical tools to investigate interesting problems in physics. I also value broadening participation in science, inspired by my experience as an online math tutor for students in rural areas. I aspire to contribute meaningfully to both research and the scientific community.

My research experience include

  • **Topological density wave (Condensed Matter Theory)** in Professor Chen-Hsuan's group: Currently focusing on theoretical analysis and numerical simulation of topological properties in these systems.
  • **Solar Neutrino Oscillation (High Energy Theory)** in Professor Meng-Ru's group: Developed a modular numerical framework to study neutrino physics, including MSW effects and exploring preliminary models for dark photon-neutrino interactions. (The numerical framework is publicly available).
  • **LIGO Data Analysis (High Energy Experiment)** in Professor Henry's group: Conducted numerical simulations and systematic parameter scans on publicly available LIGO data, focusing on glitch problem identification and data interpretation.

My academic experience include

  • Learning basic concepts about high energy physics in Professor Chuan-Ren's group
  • Learning basic concepts about black hole physics in Professor Hung-Yi's group
